The Question

Before PSG’s Qatari takeover in 2011, Ligue 1 was arguably Europe’s most unpredictable top division. Eight different clubs won the title between 1992 and 2011. Has PSG killed that unpredictability permanently?

Key Findings

TipSummary
  • Pre-PSG (1992-2011): 8 different champions in 20 seasons, the most diverse of any top-5 league
  • Post-PSG (2012-2025): PSG have won ~10 of 13 titles
  • Title-winning points jumped from 65-75 to 85-95 after the takeover
  • The 2023/24 reduction to 18 teams compressed totals but not the gap
  • Montpellier (2012), Monaco (2017), Lille (2021) are remarkable exceptions

outliers = []
# Dynamically find outlier y-values
notable = [
   ("2011/12", "Montpellier!", "#66FF66", -50, -30),
   ("2015/16", "PSG dominant", "#4FC3F7", 0, -25),
   ("2020/21", "Lille upset", "#FFD54F", -50, -30),
]
for season, text, color, ax, ay in notable:
   match = df[df["Season"] == season]
   if not match.empty:
       outliers.append({
           "season": season,
           "y": int(match["Title-Winning Points"].iloc[0]),
           "text": text,
           "color": color,
           "ax": ax,
           "ay": ay,
       })

fig = build_league_chart(df, config, eras=eras, outliers=outliers)
fig.show()

Context

  • Reduced to 18 teams in 2023/24 (the only top-5 league to shrink recently)
  • PSG’s wage bill is estimated at 3-5x the league average
  • Despite PSG, more surprise champions (Montpellier, Monaco, Lille) than the Bundesliga in the same period
  • Lyon’s pre-PSG dynasty (7 titles, 2002-2008) was achieved through academy development, not external investment
