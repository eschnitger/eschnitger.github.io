The Question

In 1992/93, Manchester United won the first Premier League title with 84 points. Coventry City survived relegation with 49. The gap: 35 points.

In 2019/20, Liverpool won with 99 points. Aston Villa survived with 34. The gap: 65 points.

Is the Premier League becoming a two-tier league?

Key Findings

TipSummary
  • Title-winning points have trended upward at roughly +0.25 points per season
  • Relegation survival points are flat or declining at roughly -0.15 points per season
  • The gap has nearly doubled, from an average of 42 pts (1990s) to 58 pts (2020s)
  • The sharpest inflection was 2016/17, when Guardiola and Klopp triggered a points arms race
  • The same 6 clubs dominate the top 4 with increasing regularity

era_stats = []
for era in eras:
   start_idx = df[df["Season"] == era["start"]].index[0]
   end_idx = df[df["Season"] == era["end"]].index[0]
   era_slice = df.iloc[start_idx:end_idx + 1]
   era_stats.append({
       "Era": era["label"].split("(")[0].strip(),
       "Seasons": len(era_slice),
       "Avg Title Pts": round(era_slice["Title-Winning Points"].mean(), 1),
       "Avg Survival Pts": round(era_slice["Relegation Survival Points"].mean(), 1),
       "Avg Gap": round(era_slice["Gap"].mean(), 1),

Methodology

NoteTechnical details

Data scraped from Wikipedia’s individual season articles. The first three seasons (1992/93 through 1994/95) had 22 teams; relegation positions are adjusted accordingly. Trend lines use per-era linear regression via numpy.polyfit. Caching avoids repeated Wikipedia requests.

Part of The European Football Divide project.

## The Question

Bayern Munich have won the Bundesliga more than any other club in history. But
has their dominance grown, or has it always been this extreme? And with the
50+1 rule supposedly ensuring competitive balance, why does Germany's league
look so predictable?

## Key Findings

::: {.callout-tip}
## Summary

- **Bayern have won ~21 of 32 titles** (66%) since 1992
- **The gap trend is flatter than the Premier League** because Bayern's dominance was already established
- **Dortmund's emergence** (2010-12) represents the only sustained challenge
- **The 18-team, 34-match format** produces lower raw point totals, but the ratio of champion to survival is comparable to other leagues
- **16th-place playoff** makes the relegation boundary more ambiguous than in other leagues
:::

Context

  • 18 teams, 34 matches per season (lower point totals than 20-team leagues)
  • 2 direct relegation + 1 playoff (16th place plays 3rd in 2. Bundesliga)
  • 50+1 ownership rule prevents majority private ownership, yet Bayern’s commercial dominance achieves a similar competitive imbalance through wages
  • Dortmund (2011, 2012) and Wolfsburg (2009) are rare interlopers
